FAQ

What information do you need from my side to get started?

To kick off smoothly, your web designer consultant will need:

  • A brief on your business/MVP/idea, target users, and goals
  • Access to any existing brand guidelines or assets (if you have)
  • Details on your current platform
  • Tech stack or CMS preferences (if known)
  • Key challenges you're facing with your current site or the specific goals you'd like to address through consultation
  • Any timelines, deadlines, or internal priorities

The more context you share upfront, the more strategic and tailored our recommendations will be. But don’t worry! If something’s missing, we’ll guide you through it.

How do you ensure my business goals are reflected in the website strategy?

We begin with a strategy workshop to align your business objectives (raising funding, improving retention, increasing signups, or entering new markets, etc.). Then, we map those goals to specific user journeys, key conversion points, and feature priorities. Every decision is tied back to your KPIs. You’ll see your business goals embedded directly into the strategy.

Will I be involved in the decision-making process?

Yes, fully. During the consultation, all attention is on your vision, needs, and challenges. If you continue with deeper services (such as UX audit, conversion-focused design strategies, design system and brand integration, product design, or MVP development), you stay informed at every step.

You’ll review key deliverables after each stage, give feedback during check-ins, and have direct communication with your product manager and designers. It’s a true partnership, not just a handoff.

Can you recommend the right tech stack for my website goals?

Absolutely. Our website development consultants evaluate your business model, scalability needs, and internal resources to recommend the most efficient and future-ready stack. We align every tech choice with your goals, team capacity, and long-term roadmap. No generic solutions! Just what’s right for your product and growth stage.

Can you assist with third-party integrations or API recommendations?

Yes. Custom recommendations are a key component of our web development consulting services. We evaluate your needs and suggest the most effective third-party tools or APIs to support your goals. If you also need help implementing those integrations, we will move into our development services.

Do you provide a roadmap or phased plan for implementation?

Of course! Our web design agency will provide you with a clear, actionable roadmap. After understanding your business goals, we break down the process into strategic phases: discovery, audit, prioritization, and execution planning.

You'll receive a step-by-step plan that outlines what to do, when to do it, and why it matters (including estimated timelines, required resources, and impact per phase). No matter if you continue with design and development implementation with us or in-house, the roadmap becomes your blueprint for confident, goal-aligned execution.

Do you offer competitive or market analysis?

Yes. Competitive and market analysis is a core part of our process. We identify your direct and indirect competitors within your niche and then analyze their user experience, visual language, product features, messaging, and market positioning.

You receive:

  • A visual and strategic comparison of top competitors
  • UX/UI reports with insights on strengths, gaps, and usability patterns
  • Strategic benchmarks for visual identity, conversion flows, onboarding, and product features
  • Clear, actionable opportunities to differentiate your product and increase market share

These insights give you a tactical advantage and help you make design, branding, and feature decisions with confidence.

Can you help migrate or modernize a legacy web infrastructure?

Sure. EliTechWiz team helps companies transition from outdated systems to modern, scalable digital platforms. First, we assess your current architecture, performance limitations, and business goals. Then, we create a phased migration plan that minimizes downtime and risk.

This includes:

  • UX/UI redesign aligned with today’s user expectations
  • Technical audit of your current stack
  • Selection of future-ready technologies and platforms
  • Roadmap for data migration, frontend rebuild, and system integration

We’ll guide you through each step with clarity and control.

Can you help align web design with our brand strategy or redesign efforts?

Yes. Your website must reflect your brand strategy! It’s a must-have thing for each business and a typical stage in our web design approach. If you need to refine an existing brand or go through a complete redesign, our expert web design team makes sure your digital presence feels consistent, strategic, and future-proof.

Let’s take a closer look.

For brand strategy alignment.
‍Our brand designers analyze your brand’s positioning, values, voice, and visual identity. Then, we translate these elements into digital experiences. We define color systems, typography, interaction patterns, and visual hierarchy, and you choose the best option. You can be sure that your website reflects who you are and what you stand for.

For redesign service.
We begin with a UX/UI audit of your current website to identify friction points, outdated visuals, and inconsistencies in brand expression. Based on real data, user feedback, and stakeholder input, we map out a redesign strategy that modernizes your interface, improves usability, and supports business growth. Following your feedback, our redesign team will begin working on it.

The result you’ll get? A cohesive, conversion-ready site that tells your story with clarity and builds trust across every touchpoint.

Do you provide wireframes, mockups, or prototypes during consulting?

Not exactly. Our consulting services focus on expert analysis, guidance, and strategic recommendations through discovery calls, basic audits, and documentation. The main goal of our consultation is to help you make informed decisions before you commit to design or development and avoid costly rework. Just show the experience-proofed vision.

It requires significantly more time than the duration of the consultation. So, while we can’t deliver visual assets like wireframes or prototypes during this stage, we’ll be happy to show you what those would look like and how they could work for your product in the next steps.

If you’d like to move forward beyond consulting, here’s what each design step includes:

  • Wireframes. These are low-fidelity, black-and-white layouts that show the structure and content flow of your pages. You’ll see where buttons, content blocks, and user flows go. It’s without colors or branding. Wireframes' goal is to validate your product logic and UX early on.
  • Mockups. These are high-fidelity static screens with your brand visuals, typography, colors, and content. They show exactly what your product will look like and are perfect for stakeholder approvals or investor pitches.
  • Prototypes. These are interactive versions of your mockups. You can click through the flows like a real user would. It's an ideal option for usability testing, stakeholder feedback, and development handoff.

Want to see how strategy turns into design? We’d be happy to support you with a full-cycle product design service once the consulting phase is complete.
