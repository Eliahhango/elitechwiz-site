FAQ

What types of financial institutions do you typically work with?

We work with a wide range of financial companies, from early-stage fintech startups to established institutions looking to modernize. That includes:

  • Neobanks
  • Payment platforms
  • DeFi apps
  • Investment tools
  • Insurance tech
  • Financial automation products

Building your first MVP or scaling a platform with millions in transactions? Our team will deliver results that drive growth and inspire trust. Just book a free call and learn more about us.

Do you work with fintech startups as well as established firms?

Yes! If you're a startup, we help you launch quickly with a brand and product that looks credible from the very beginning. Something investors and users can trust.

If you're scaling or already established, we support you with redesigns, design systems, UX improvements, and dev parts that modernize your platform without disrupting operations.

If you're pre-seed or post-Series C, our team knows how to adapt to your stage, speed, and goals to deliver a design that drives growth at every level.

How long does a typical branding or web design project take?

It depends on the scope, but here’s a general breakdown.

For simpler projects:

  • Branding (logo, colors, typography, basic style guide): 3 weeks
  • Web design: 4-8 weeks

For more complex projects:

  • Full branding package (strategy, visual identity, brand system): 5 weeks
  • Multi-page website with custom UX, CMS, and dev: 8+ weeks

No matter the size, you’ll get a clear timeline, weekly or daily updates, and a dedicated project manager to keep things running smoothly. Launch with confidence and focus on growth.

For more information, contact us.

Can you help modernize a legacy financial brand?

Absolutely. Our team works closely with your leadership, marketing, and compliance teams to refresh your visual identity, refine your messaging, and improve your digital presence across all touchpoints.

You’ll get a modern, scalable brand system that feels current, credible, and aligned with today’s fintech standards.

Just think: stronger positioning, cleaner design, and a better experience for users and internal teams.

If your goal is to stay competitive and relevant, a thoughtful rebrand is often the smartest move. And our financial services creative agency is here to lead it.

How do you tailor your design approach for financial service providers?

We understand that financial products come with high expectations. That’s why we built our approach around your goals, your team, and the people who use your product every day.

We start by learning about your product, your compliance landscape, and your users' actual behavior. Then, we focus on designing for clarity, trust, and conversion.

For simpler projects:

  • Clear UX that simplifies complex financial flows
  • Secure, trustworthy design that builds user confidence
  • Compliance-aware decisions
  • Data-led user flows focused on reducing friction and improving retention
  • Scalable systems that grow with your product and team

How do you ensure a financial brand feels both modern and trustworthy?

It’s all about balance, which we’ve refined through 9+ years of working with fintech startups and financial institutions. A modern brand grabs attention, but a trustworthy one holds it. We combine clean, forward-thinking visuals with strategic design choices that signal stability, security, and professionalism.

Here’s how we do it:

  • Typography and color palettes that feel fresh but grounded
  • Minimal, intentional UI that communicates clarity and control
  • Visual consistency across web, product, pitch decks, and collateral
  • Brand voice and messaging that reflects confidence and transparency
  • UX decisions that make users feel in control and safe with their data

Can you design dashboards or data-heavy interfaces for financial platforms?

​​Of course, designing clean, user-friendly dashboards and data-rich interfaces is one of our core strengths, especially in the financial space. We turn complex information (transactions, analytics, investment data, or reporting) into intuitive, easy-to-navigate experiences and make sure your users can take action quickly and never feel overwhelmed.

Here’s what we focus on:

  • Smart data hierarchy
  • Modular dashboards tailored to different user roles
  • Clean visualizations
  • Secure, accessible UI
  • Responsive layouts

How do you ensure project transparency and on-time delivery?

You get a dedicated project manager who keeps communication clear and timelines on track. We use Slack, Google Meets, Figma, and Notion to provide real-time updates, share progress, gather feedback, and align priorities. No chasing, no surprises.

Here’s what that looks like in practice:

  • Detailed project roadmap
  • Weekly check-ins
  • Clear documentation
  • Agile delivery

Our goal is to deliver high-quality work and make the entire process smooth, predictable, and easy to manage on your end.

Do you offer competitor analysis or market research as part of your branding process?

Yes, competitor analysis and market research are integral to our branding process from the outset. To position your financial product effectively, we need to understand the landscape you’re entering (or disrupting).

Our team analyzes your direct and indirect competitors, assesses industry trends, identifies brand gaps, and pinpoints opportunities to differentiate (visually, strategically, and emotionally). Our research provides a clear picture of your current position and shows how to stand out.

Here are our typical steps:

  • Competitor visual audits
  • Market positioning maps to highlight white space
  • User expectation insights for your product category

How do you measure the effectiveness of your design work?

Our team measures success by how well the design performs. From the start, we align with your business goals and track key metrics, including conversion rates, user retention, engagement, bounce rates, activation, and even fundraising outcomes.

We partner with you to make sure the work performs, and we’re always ready to iterate based on real data.

Here’s how the team typically measures effectiveness:

  • Before-and-after comparisons on KPIs (e.g., signup rates, onboarding completion, or time on platform)
  • User feedback from surveys, interviews, or in-app ratings
  • Behavioral analytics via tools (Hotjar, Mixpanel, or GA4) to track how users interact with the new design
  • A/B testing for different layouts, content, or flows
  • Business outcomes (e.g., reduced support tickets, increased demo requests, or improved investor traction)
