FAQ

Will my current website stay live during the redesign process?

Absolutely! You don’t need to worry about the operations. Your existing website remains live and fully functional while the redesign is in progress.

Here's how our web redesign company typically works:
  • We build the new version of your site in a secure staging environment.  This is a private space for all the design,  content,  and development updates visible only to design and development teams.  This way, your visitors can continue using the current website without any interruptions,  downtime,  or broken features.
  • Once the redesign is complete,  tested,  and approved,  we seamlessly switch over to the new version.  We always do it during off-peak hours to minimize any risk of disruption.  You’ll always have full access and visibility throughout the process.

Can you redesign my site without changing the content?

Of course! If you like your current content and it matches your tone of voice everywhere,  we can redesign a website only.  The main focus is purely on improving the visual design, structure,  and user experience (without altering your text,  images,  or key messaging). In this case,  the main goal is to present your existing content more effectively.  We optimize layouts, typography,  and navigation to make the information easier to read,  more engaging,  and more aligned with modern design standards. During the process, our professional team might also suggest minor adjustments if something could improve user flow or conversion rates.  BUT the final decision is always yours, and you stay fully in control of what gets updated.

How long does a redesign typically take?

The timeline for a website redesign services depends on the scope of work and the complexity:

  • How many web pages your website has.
  • If you need custom graphics,  illustrations,  or motion.
  • What about additional sections to meet the market,  SEO,  accessibility,  and regulation requirements,  etc.

As a professional web redesign agency, our team always adjusts the process based on what’s needed to meet your goals without rushing quality.

Here’s a closer look at the possibilities:

1.  Simpler website redesign (estimated 3+ weeks)

If your goal is mainly to refresh the layout, update the visual style,  improve usability,  and align the design with your current brand identity.  But without big changes.  The process looks like this:

  • Discovery & research to understand your audience, brand,  and current UX problems.
  • Wireframing key pages to plan the structure and navigation.
  • UI design,  where we apply a modernized look (colors,  fonts,  layouts) and keep everything clean and user-friendly.
  • Final adjustments to make refinements based on your feedback.
2.  Complex website redesign (estimated 7+ weeks)

It's the ideal solution if you want a full transformation with custom graphics,  bespoke illustrations,  motion design,  interactive elements,  or a totally new brand experience.  The process in this case looks like:

  • Discovery & strategy with deeper brand positioning,  competitor research,  and user persona development.
  • Advanced wireframing to plan interactive flows,  animations,  and rich content areas.
  • UI design to apply all elements cohesively across different pages and devices.
  • Final refinements.

Can you align the redesign with my existing brand identity?

Yes,  because it’s one of the most important parts of a successful website redesign!

If you already have a strong brand identity (logo,  color palette,  typography,  and tone of voice),  our expert will make sure the new website feels like a natural extension of it.

Here’s how our process works:
  • Brand audit to review your current brand assets and fully understand your visual style,  messaging,  and personality.
  • Design adaptation to apply your existing colors,  fonts,  and design elements to the new layouts.
  • Consistency across touchpoints for trust and recognition across devices.  It means that every page, interaction,  and visual element is in line with your brand.

And if you feel your brand identity could use a few updates, we can help with that,  too.  For example,  refresh the color palette,  tweak typography,  or refine graphic elements.

Will my redesigned site be mobile-friendly and responsive?

Absolutely,  you can expect your new site to be fully optimized for all devices (smartphones,  tablets,  laptops,  and desktops).  Mobile responsiveness is a standard (not an extra feature).

Here’s how we approach it:
  • Mobile-first thinking.  We design with mobile users in mind from the very beginning so that layouts,  typography,  buttons,  and visuals work seamlessly on smaller screens.
  • Consistent user experience.  We keep proportion,  readability,  and usability across every screen size.  Users will enjoy the same elegant,  intuitive experience whether they visit from a phone or a desktop.
  • Testing across devices.  Before launch,  we rigorously test the website across multiple devices and browsers.
Why does responsiveness matter?

Today,  63% of website traffic comes from mobile devices.  A mobile-friendly,  responsive design is critical for user engagement,  SEO performance,  and your brand’s credibility.

Can I see wireframes or prototypes before development begins?

Sure! It's a crucial part of the process.  You will have full real-time access to every document and file our team works on.

Before any development starts,  we always share wireframes and,  in many cases,  clickable prototypes.  It gives you full visibility and control and ensures that the foundation is solid before we invest time in detailed design or development.  It also allows for faster feedback and adjustments to save time and resources later.

Here’s how it works:
  • Wireframes.  These are simple, black-and-white layouts that map out the page structure, content hierarchy,  and navigation.  Wireframes focus on usability and information flow.  They help you understand how users will move through the site.
  • Prototypes.  In more complex projects, we also create interactive prototypes.  These allow you to click through pages and experience basic user flows.  They make it easier to spot potential issues or opportunities early.

For small projects,  wireframes are enough,  but for more significant projects,  we recommend requesting prototypes.

Do you offer dark mode or other modern UI features?

Of course, we follow the latest news and insights to bring your industry's latest UI trends and technologies to your website.

Here’s what we can integrate based on your goals:

  • Dark mode.  We design a fully optimized dark mode and ensure comfort,  style,  and perfect readability for users who prefer darker interfaces.
  • Microinteractions.  Subtle animations (button hovers,  form feedback,  loading indicators,  etc.)  create a more intuitive and engaging user experience.
  • Scroll-based animations.  Animations that trigger as users scroll down the page help guide attention,  tell your brand story dynamically,  and make the experience feel immersive.
  • Sticky navigation and smart headers.  Sticky menus,  dynamic headers that hide and reveal on scroll,  or mobile-optimized bottom nav bars improve navigation and user comfort.  It’s especially important on mobile devices.
  • Dynamic content loading.  Lazy loading images,  smooth content transitions,  and modular sections ensure faster page loads and better performance without sacrificing a rich visual experience.
  • Interactive cards and sections.  Hover reveals,  flip cards,  or interactive service sections that respond to user actions.  They add depth without overwhelming the core journey.
  • Custom illustrations and animated elements.  Custom graphic elements,  animated icons,  or light motion graphics bring personality to your brand and make your website more competitive.
  • Accessibility-first features.  We also incorporate accessibility standards (contrast adjustments,  keyboard navigation,  readable font sizes) so that your website is usable for everyone and meets all regulation requirements.

Key point! Dark mode and modern features enhance visual appeal,  but we prioritize performance and usability.  Our experts will tell you what your website needs to win the competition race and what is just a trend.  Every solution is optimized to keep the site fast,  accessible,  and easy to navigate.
So,  you will get a future-ready,  highly engaging user experience that balances beauty,  performance,  and accessibility.

How much does a typical website redesign cost?

When you invest in a strategic redesign,  you’re investing in stronger business results (not just better visuals).  The cost of a website redesign services can vary widely.  It depends mainly on the scope of work,  the complexity of your website,  and the website redesigning services you choose.

Here’s a general breakdown:
  • Basic redesign ($5,000–$10,000). 
    This covers a visual refresh of a smaller website (up to 5–7 pages) with updated layouts,  colors,  typography,  and basic UX improvements.  It’s without adding complex custom graphics,  illustrations,  or animations.  This option is ideal if you want to modernize your site’s look but keep the existing structure mostly intact.
  • Standard redesign ($10,000–$20,000).  
    This range typically includes a deeper UX overhaul,  custom UI design for key pages,  mobile optimization,  better content structure,  and minor brand identity updates if needed. It suits companies that want a more strategic redesign.
  • Complex or custom redesign ($20,000+).
    This service is when you require extensive UX strategy,  custom illustrations,  motion design,  advanced animations,  multi-language support,  interactive elements,  or partial brand redefinition.  The project becomes more sophisticated, and the cost adjusts accordingly.
What affects the price?
  • Number of pages and templates.
  • Custom UX strategy and research.
  • Unique visuals (illustrations,  custom icons,  infographics).
  • Motion design and animations.
  • Content migration or updates.
  • Mobile-first and accessibility enhancements.
  • Integration with platforms (CMS,  CRM,  booking tools,  etc.).

What if I only want a partial redesign — is that possible?

Yes, a partial redesign is absolutely possible.  
If your brand identity,  core structure,  or major pages are already performing well,  you don’t always need a full website overhaul.  We can focus only on specific areas that need improvement without disrupting what’s already working.

Here’s how a partial redesign typically works:
  • Targeted updates. We focus on refreshing specific parts of your website (e.g.,  the homepage,  key service pages,  or checkout flow).
  • Visual alignment.  We make sure any new designs blend seamlessly with your existing style and branding,  so users experience a consistent journey across old and new sections.
  • User-first focus. We prioritize areas that impact user experience,  engagement,  or conversion rates the most.
  • Flexible scaling. If needed,  the redesign can be expanded later.  You can start small and gradually upgrade other parts of the site when you're ready.
Common examples of partial redesigns:
  • Redesigning the homepage to make a stronger first impression.
  • Updating service/product pages to improve conversions.
  • Refreshing outdated blogs or resource sections for better engagement.
  • Improving mobile responsiveness without touching desktop layouts.
  • Enhancing visual consistency across select high-traffic pages.

Do you offer a design-first approach or development-first approach?

We always recommend a design-first approach because strategy,  user experience,  and visual communication should guide development.  Not the other way around!

The design-first approach starts with research to understand your brand,  your users,  and your goals.  We create wireframes,  structure the user flows,  craft visual designs,  and refine the user experience.  Only after that do we move into development.  It means that every feature,  layout,  and interaction is intentional and matches your users and your business objectives.

Some agencies or developers prefer a development-first approach.  They start coding immediately and adjust the design as they go.  It’s faster at first but leads to messy user experiences,  inconsistent branding,  limited flexibility,  and costly fixes later on.
